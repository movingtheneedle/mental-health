what is mental health?

according to the world health organization, mental health is a state of well-being that allows people to do human things - it allows them to handle their life, explore themselves, and contribute to a community. it's a person's mental condition. mental health is important to understand how we work psychologically. a poor mental health results in differed thoughts, feelings, and behavior, and can cause people to be unsure of how to deal with them.

types of mental issues

this can vary from anxiety disorders (which result in excessive fear, worry, and panic attacks), depression (loss of pleasure/interest, sad/irritable mood), bipolar disorder (depressed mood or manic symptoms such as euphoria), post-traumatic stress disorder (intrusive flashbacks/memories), schizophrenzia/psychosis/paranoia (hallucinations, disorganization, agitation), eating disorders (such as anorexia, bulimia, binge-eating - irregular eating habits), obsessive-compulsive disorder (repeated thoughts/urges), disruptive behavior/dissocial disorders (defiancy), neurodevelopmental disorders (autism, attention deficit hyperactivity disorder - difficulty in communication, focusing). there's a lot of varieties because, afterall, humans have a spectrum of emotions.
